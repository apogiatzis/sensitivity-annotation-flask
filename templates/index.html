Context-aware deep sensitivity annotation on textual data

Author: Antreas Pogiatzis
Supervisor: Dr. Inah Omoronyia
Abstract:

Identifying sensitive information is a crucial intermediatestep in privacy-preserving applications. However, privacyand more specifically the concept of ”sensitive information”is an extremely elusive term which is heavily depended uponthe context that is conveyed in. This paper presents a predic-tive context-aware model based on a Bidirectional Long ShortTerm Memory network which can perform multi-class sensi-tivity labelling. We train our model on a synthetic annotateddataset and conduct a series of experiments which mark theadoption of a BiLSTM+CRF model architecture with BERTembeddings and WordShape features as the most optimal forsuch a task with an F1 score of 96.86%. Moreover, an evalu-ation of the proposed model is conducted under different con-text dimensions. In this regard, our proposed model achievesa 73.62% F1 score on unseen data and outperforms Google’sData Loss Prevention (DLP) system sensitivity annotationswith a relative accuracy improvement of 42.65%.
